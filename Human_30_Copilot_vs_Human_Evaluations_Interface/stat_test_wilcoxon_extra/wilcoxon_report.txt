### Test: One-Sample Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test
  H₀: median preference score = 0 (no systematic preference)
  H₁: median ≠ 0 (two-sided)
  Zero handling: 'wilcox' method — scores of 0 are discarded from ranking
                 (classical treatment for genuine ties)
  Effect size: r = Z / √N_nonzero  (ranges −1 to +1)
  Field-level FDR correction: Benjamini-Hochberg (BH)

## 5. Interpretation Notes
  - The Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test is non-parametric and does not assume
    normality. It tests whether the median score distribution is symmetric
    around zero.
  - Effect size r ≈ 0.1 small, 0.3 medium, 0.5 large (Cohen 1988).
  - For field-level tests, BH-adjusted p-values control the false discovery
    rate at 5% across the 21 simultaneous comparisons.
  - Per-publication tests use n=21 observations each; low power for
    individual publications; interpret as exploratory.
